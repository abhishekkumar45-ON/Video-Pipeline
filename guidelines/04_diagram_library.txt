How to read a spec
One spec per diagram type. Each spec is the single source of truth for how that diagram looks in every video, thumbnail and card — and later becomes a component in
nelumbo_diagrams.py
, built to match this page pixel for pixel.
Every spec has four parts
1 · Reference render
— the diagram in its canonical state, drawn to the frame-construction law. What you see is what ships.
2 · Construction
— strokes, weights, geometry rules, and how the diagram is built on screen (draw order for the eventual Create() sequence).
3 · Colour duty
— which parts are semantic (fixed everywhere) and which parts take the chapter's combination.
4 · States
— the named states the diagram must support (rest → annotated → resolved), so beats transition it instead of redrawing it.
Universal law — binding on every spec
SCAFFOLD titanium 3px
ACTIVE/TARGET ignition 4px
GIVEN cyan
CONFIRMED green, once
WRONG red only
LABELS Mono/Body ≥28px, never rotated >±30°
FILLS graphite or chapter secondary ≤20%
BUILT ONCE, EVOLVED · NO COLLISIONS · PAUSE-TEST CLEAN
The chapter's combination appears in exactly two places inside a diagram: the
chapter badge
and
non-semantic surfaces
(media, regions, instrument bodies). Anything that carries meaning keeps the semantic palette — a given is cyan in every chapter of every subject, forever.
Living document.
Chapters are added in syllabus order and locked once used in production. Colour assignments come from the Chapter Colour Map — signed off, and changed only with a re-render of that chapter's assets.

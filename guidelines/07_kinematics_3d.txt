// 02 — Depth & Colour
Reading depth
3D can confuse fast. Four depth cues keep it legible while the colour law carries over: cyan for motion, orange for acceleration, titanium for the scaffold.
Ground grid
A faint x–y
NumberPlane
on the floor anchors everything. Depth is obvious the moment there's a grid to sit on.
Dropated shadow
Project the moving object straight down to the floor as a dim dot — the shadow tells height at a glance.
Fade with distance
Gentle orbit
A slow ambient rotation resolves any ambiguity — parallax reveals the true shape.
v
grid + shadow + component vectors
Rule —
never show 3D without at least a ground grid and a shadow. If depth is still unclear after 2 seconds, add a slow orbit — don't add more labels.

Choreography & render
The camera is the narrator in 3D. A tight set of moves, and a few settings that keep 3D renders fast enough to iterate.

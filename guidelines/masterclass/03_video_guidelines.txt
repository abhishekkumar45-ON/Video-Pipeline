01 — Foundations
The pipeline
Three documents run every Masterclass video. Each owns one thing. This book is the third: it owns what the screen looks like — nothing more, nothing less.
Script agent
· ON masterclass script instructions
Owns WORDS and STRUCTURE. Writes the master timing document: segments, walls, blocks, word budgets, SCREEN intents. Names objects by role, never by look.
Frame progression agent
· ON masterclass frame instructions
Owns TIMING, SEQUENCE, SYNC and STATE. Converts script plus word-level audio timestamps into the progression manifest: every element's birth, life and death, pinned to spoken words.
This book
· ON video guidelines
Division of authority
What is said, when
Script agent. Nobody downstream rewrites narration.
What appears, on which frame
Frame agent. Anchors, durations, state snapshots.
What it looks like, how it moves
This book. Obeyed by the renderer without further decisions.
Conflicts
Escalate upstream with the exact reference. Never patch by bending a rule.
Naming is law.

Colour on video
The brand palette becomes a grammar: on video, colour is meaning first and decoration never. Each colour has exactly one job, so the learner reads state without thinking.
The current subject. Singular — one element at a time, per Law 3.
Kickers, labels, secondary accents, resolved agenda ticks.
Given data: known values, given conditions, annotations carried from the question.
Constructions and text at rest — everything already discussed.
Confirmed results, the correct option, sanity checks that pass.
Rejected branches, eliminated options, the trap's visible break.
Edge-case markers, boundary conditions, guard rules.
Surfaces
Rules
Ratio
80 / 15 / 5 holds on video: dark surfaces dominate, white and titanium carry content, ignition is scarce.
Cooling
When narration moves on, the previous subject cools ignition → titanium over 400 ms. Nothing stays orange out of turn.
Never
White text on ignition. Small orange body text. Cyan for emphasis. More than one state colour on a single element.
Contrast
All narration-critical text ≥ AA on its surface. White on carbon 15.8:1 · titanium on carbon 9.4:1 · ember on obsidian 6.1:1.

Reactivation

The return — payoff beat
The five-move sequence
t+0ms
Carrybacks detach from the block and hold. Block space begins its collapse into the agenda entry.
+500ms
Agenda resolves

≤2 sent.
The step resolves
The blocked step executes in ONE visible move — one line entering the stack, one construction completing — with the carryback card flashing as its source. Fast is the design: this is the payoff.
Speed budget
Collapse-to-resolution must complete inside the two-sentence window the script guarantees. If the audio gap is tight, moves overlap harder — the sequence compresses, it never truncates.
The one-line moment
a cut back to the canvas, a re-drawn canvas, a return without the agenda tick, or a resolution that dribbles across three moves. One collapse, one restore, one line.

Long questions & overflow
When even QC-C cannot hold the question at minimum sizes, the read splits — the text never shrinks below spec and never scrolls.
The two-stage read
Stage 1 · stem
The scenario text alone, full frame, read with the underline sweep. When the read reaches the actual ask, the scenario compresses upward to 60% height…
Stage 2 · ask + options
…and the ask plus options assemble in the lower 40%, options on their anchors as always. The stem stays visible at 32 px — dim, but present.
Countdown
Other overflow rules
Comprehension passages
Long option sets
Options over 12 words go single-column, and if all four cannot fit, they enter two at a time as read, earlier pair receding to 60% — never leaving the frame entirely.
Matrix-match
shrink below the 28 px floor, scroll text, paraphrase the question on screen, or crop the figure. The question appears exactly as the paper asked it, or the format changes.

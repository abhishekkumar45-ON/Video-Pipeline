Orange Nelumbo · Diagram Library
Chapter colour map
Every chapter of the JEE syllabus is assigned one combination from the brand's 25 curated sets. The combination is the chapter's
identity
— it colours the scaffold and packaging, never the meaning. Combinations repeat across subjects (25 combos, 60 chapters) but are unique within each subject.
What the chapter combination controls
Chapter badge & eyebrow tint
— the topic chip on question cards, thumbnail eyebrows, series cards and playlist art for that chapter.
Diagram secondary family
— fills, media, field regions, shaded areas and other non-semantic surfaces inside that chapter's diagrams.
Recap-card & top-up accents
— the chapter's cards carry its combo as border/fill tints, so a feed of revision cards reads as a syllabus.
In-app chapter surfaces
— chapter headers, progress tiles and any future chapter-scoped asset.
What it never touches — the fixed semantic layer
WHITE — working math
SIGNAL — givens & telemetry
IGNITION — the active move
TITANIUM — scaffold & ghosts
CORRECT — the answer, once
ERROR — traps & misfires
AMBER — trigger words, READ only
Chapter grouping.
The syllabus is consolidated to 20 chapters per subject (JEE Main + Advanced). If your curriculum splits differently, sub-chapters inherit their parent's combination — the map stays stable.

05 — In-Video Graphics
Intro sting
2.5 seconds, after the cold-open hook — never before it. The mark ignites, the series card states the mission, we cut to content.
0.0–0.6s
Mark fades in from black at 35%, ring begins a slow clockwise sweep.
0.6–1.4s
Ignition: glow blooms behind the mark, single low engine-thrum note.
MISSION LOG · 014
AIR 247, EXPLAINED
1.4–2.5s
Series card slides in from left; hard cut to content on the beat.
Rules
Hook first.
The sting never opens the video — 5–15 seconds of cold-open content run before it. Viewers decide in the first 5 seconds; give them substance, not a logo.
2.5s hard cap.
Shorts skip the sting entirely — the watermark carries the brand there.
One sound.
The engine-thrum ignition note is the channel's audio logo. No music bed under the sting.

06 — Titles & Metadata
Titles
The thumbnail carries emotion; the title carries information. They must never say the same thing. Formula:
outcome + specificity + constraint.
Rules
≤ 55 characters — nothing truncates in the feed. Front-load the keyword in the first 30.
Sentence case. Caps only for series prefixes and exam names (JEE, AIR, NTA).
Numbers as digits — "7 mistakes", never "seven". Real numbers only; every figure defensible.
No clickbait grammar: no ALL-CAPS words, no "(GONE WRONG)", max one "?" and zero "!".
Patterns by pillar
P1 PROOF
How Ananya went from 71 to 96 percentile in 14 weeks
P2 TEACH
Rotational mechanics: the 3-step method for every torque problem
P3 LAUNCH
Your JEE rank, predicted live — inside the new telemetry engine
P4 OFFER
The 2026 scholarship window: who qualifies and how to apply
P5 T-MINUS
60 days to JEE Main: the only revision plan you need
The pairing test.
Read thumbnail and title together. If either repeats the other, one of them is wasted. Thumbnail: "AIR 247". Title: how it happened.

Fixed skeleton, filled per video. The first 125 characters show before the fold — they are a second title, not a summary.

HOOK
Two sentences, mission-control voice, a number in the first line.
ONE LINK
A single trial link above the fold. Link dumps dilute clicks and trust.
CHAPTERS
Mandatory over 6 minutes. Chapter names use lesson language, not "part 1".
HASHTAGS
Exactly three: #JEE[year] + subject/series + #OrangeNelumbo.
Compliance.
Every results claim in a description needs a real, verifiable student behind it. No "guaranteed rank" language anywhere — see the Legal chapter of the Brand Guidelines.

07 — Motion & Pacing
Motion principles
Graphics move like instruments, not confetti: fast in, precise stop, quiet exit. Everything eases on the launch curve.
Launch curve
cubic-bezier(.2, .8, .2, 1)
The one easing for every graphic entrance and exit. Hard thrust, soft dock. No bounce, no elastic, no spring.
Durations
Chips / captions   200–300ms
Lower thirds     400ms
Count-ups        600ms
Sting ignition   800ms
Direction
Text enters from the left (reading direction). Telemetry fades and counts in place. Nothing rotates, nothing zooms past 105%, nothing shakes.
Restraint
Max one animated graphic at a time. If footage and a graphic compete, the graphic waits. Motion signals precision — it never begs for attention.
Transitions between scenes
HARD CUT
Default. 95% of all edits.
DIP TO OBSIDIAN
Chapter changes only, 300ms.
EVERYTHING ELSE
Wipes, spins, glitches, zooms — banned.

Structure is fixed; the clock changes by pillar. Every long-form video opens with a cold-open hook and closes with the 20-second end card.

The clock, by pillar
P1 PROOF
10–15 min · conversational cuts 6–10s · hook = the rank on screen in second one
P2 TEACH
8–20 min · cuts 3–6s during solving · hook = the problem, posed to the viewer
P3 LAUNCH
3–6 min · product-led cuts 2–4s · hook = the feature doing the impossible thing
P4 OFFER
≤ 90s · brisk 2–3s cuts · terms on screen long enough to read aloud twice
P5 T-MINUS

Shorts:
hook ≤ 2s, one idea, loop-friendly ending (last frame answers the first). No end card — pinned comment carries the link.
Music:
low-energy electronic beds, −24 LUFS under voice. No trending audio on long-form; Shorts may use it when native to the format.

Series & naming
Five named series map one-to-one onto the pillars. The series name appears in the thumbnail eyebrow, the sting card and the playlist title — identically.
MISSION LOG

FLIGHT PLAN
P2 · strategy & planning: schedules, revision systems, exam-day protocol.

T-MINUS
P5 · countdown checkpoints keyed to the JEE calendar.
Playlist naming
Format
DRILL — Maths · Calculus
MISSION LOG — 2026 results
T-MINUS — JEE Main 2027
Rules
Series name first, em-dash, scope
Descriptions: one sentence, mission-control voice
Playlist order: curriculum order, not upload order
New series names require a brand review — they are product names, not video titles. Retired series stay listed but pinned closed.

## Question
A thermally isolated container of length L is split by a thermally CONDUCTING, movable piston of
area A. Left side holds 3/2 mol, right side 1 mol of ideal gas. On the left a spring (constant k,
natural length 2L/5) ties the piston to the wall. At equilibrium the piston sits L/2 from each edge.
The right-compartment pressure is P = (kL/A)·α. Find α.

## KNOWN ANSWER (anchor to this): α = 0.2

## Concept-first
- Conducting piston ⇒ both gases share the SAME temperature T.
- Ideal gas: P = nRT/V.
- Spring force = k·(extension), and the piston is in mechanical equilibrium (net force 0).

## LIVE VISUAL
Draw the container: left gas "3/2 mol", right gas "1 mol", the movable piston at the middle (L/2
each side), and the spring coil tying the piston to the left wall. Mark the force arrows on the
piston (P_L A →, ← P_R A, ← spring kx). No numeric ValueTracker is required here; a clean labelled
force-balance diagram is the "visual". Dock it bottom-right after the concept.

## STEP RAIL (LAW → SUBSTITUTION → RESULT)
1. Same T (conducting) + ideal gas, each side volume A·L/2:
   `P_L = (3/2 RT)/(A L/2) = 3RT/(AL)`,  `P_R = (RT)/(A L/2) = 2RT/(AL)` → **P_L − P_R = RT/(AL)**.
2. Spring extension `x = L/2 − 2L/5 = L/10`; the stretched spring pulls the piston left with
   `k x = kL/10`. → **spring force = kL/10**.
3. Force balance `P_L A = P_R A + kL/10` → `(P_L − P_R) A = kL/10` → `(RT/(AL))·A = kL/10`
   → **RT = kL²/10**.
4. Evaluate `P_R = 2RT/(AL) = (2/(AL))·(kL²/10) = kL/(5A) = (kL/A)(1/5)` → **α = 0.2**
   (results rail: `\alpha = 0.2`, CORRECT green).

Answer lock-in: **α = 0.2**.

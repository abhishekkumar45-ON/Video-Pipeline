## Question
A mixture of 5 mol monatomic gas + 1 mol rigid diatomic gas, initially at P₀, V₀, T₀, is
adiabatically compressed to V₀/4. Given 2^1.2 = 2.3, 2^3.2 = 9.2. Which statements are correct?
- (A) The work |W| done in the process is 13 R T₀
- (B) The average kinetic energy after compression is between 18 R T₀ and 19 R T₀
- (C) The final pressure is between 9 P₀ and 10 P₀
- (D) The adiabatic constant of the mixture is 1.6

## KNOWN ANSWER (anchor to this): (A), (C), (D) correct  [with T_f = 2.3 T₀; (B) is the trap]

## Concept-first
- Mixture: C_V = Σn_i C_{V,i} / Σn_i ; monatomic C_V = 3/2 R, rigid diatomic C_V = 5/2 R; C_P = C_V + R.
- Adiabatic: T V^(γ−1) = const and P V^γ = const.
- Adiabatic (Q = 0): |W| = |ΔU| = n C_V |ΔT|.

Draw a steep adiabatic P–V curve (IGNITION) and a piston compressing V₀ → V₀/4. Live readouts

Dock bottom-right after.

## STEP RAIL (LAW → SUBSTITUTION → RESULT)
1. Mixture heat capacities: `C_V = (5·(3/2 R) + 1·(5/2 R))/6 = (15/2 + 5/2)/6 R = 5R/3`,
   `C_P = C_V + R = 8R/3`.
2. Adiabatic exponent `γ = C_P/C_V = (8/3)/(5/3) = 1.6` (γ−1 = 0.6) → **(D) correct**.
3. Final temperature `T_f = T₀ · 4^0.6 = T₀ · 2^1.2 = 2.3 T₀`.
4. Final pressure `P_f = P₀ · 4^1.6 = P₀ · 2^3.2 = 9.2 P₀` (between 9 and 10 P₀) → **(C) correct**.
5. Work (Q=0): `|W| = |ΔU| = n C_V |ΔT| = 6·(5R/3)(2.3T₀ − T₀) = 10R·(1.3T₀) = 13 R T₀` → **(A) correct**.
   (B) is wrong (the total internal energy after compression ≠ 18–19 R T₀). 
   Results rail chips: `\gamma=1.6`, `P_f=9.2P_0`, `|W|=13RT_0` (CORRECT green); mark (B) ERROR red.

## Question (List matching)
Match each black-body temperature (List-I) to a statement (List-II).
Constants: Wien b = 2.9×10⁻³ m·K,  hc/e = 1.24×10⁻⁶ V·m.
List-I: (P) 2000 K, (Q) 3000 K, (R) 5000 K, (S) 10000 K.
List-II: (1) peak radiation ejects photoelectrons from a 4 eV metal; (2) peak is visible to the eye;
(3) peak gives the widest single-slit central maximum; (4) power/area = 1/16 of a body at 6000 K;
(5) peak can image bones.

## KNOWN ANSWER (anchor to this): (C)  P→3, Q→4, R→2, S→1

## Concept-first
- Wien's law: λ_peak = b/T (hotter ⇒ shorter peak wavelength).
- Stefan: power/area ∝ T⁴.
- Diffraction central max width ∝ λ; photoemission needs λ ≤ hc/(eφ).

peaks marked. A small λ-axis with the four computed peaks labelled. Then clear before the solve.

## STEP RAIL (LAW → SUBSTITUTION → RESULT)
1. Peaks via `λ = b/T`: P → 1450 nm (IR), Q → 967 nm (near-IR), R → 580 nm (visible),
   S → 290 nm (UV).
2. Match:
   - Widest single-slit central max ⇒ largest λ ⇒ **P → 3**.
   - `T⁴` ratio 1/16 vs 6000 K ⇒ T = 6000/2 = 3000 K ⇒ **Q → 4**.
   - Visible peak (580 nm) ⇒ **R → 2**.
   - Photoelectrons from 4 eV metal need `λ ≤ hc/(4e) = 310 nm`; only S (290 nm) qualifies ⇒ **S → 1**.

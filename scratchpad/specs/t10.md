## Question
One mole of a monatomic ideal gas expands adiabatically so its volume becomes 8× the initial value.
Initial temperature 100 K, R = 8.0 J·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹. Find the DECREASE in internal energy (J).

## KNOWN ANSWER (anchor to this): decrease in U = 900 J

## Concept-first
- Adiabatic (monatomic): γ = 5/3, γ−1 = 2/3; T V^(γ−1) = const.
- Internal energy: ΔU = n C_V ΔT, with C_V = 3/2 R.
- Adiabatic expansion cools the gas (it does work with no heat in) ⇒ U drops.

## LIVE VISUAL
Draw a steep adiabatic P–V curve (IGNITION) and a piston expanding V → 8V. Live readouts
(ValueTracker + DecimalNumber): V (in V₀) 1 → 8, and T (K) falling 100 → 25. Dock bottom-right after.

## STEP RAIL (LAW → SUBSTITUTION → RESULT)
1. Final temperature `T_f = T_i (V_i/8V_i)^(2/3) = 100 / 8^(2/3) = 100/4 = 25 K`.
2. Internal-energy change `ΔU = n C_V ΔT = 1·(3/2)R·(25 − 100) = (3/2)(8.0)(−75) = 12·(−75) = −900 J`.
3. So U DECREASES by **900 J** (the gas does 900 J of work adiabatically).
   (results rail: `T_f = 25\ \mathrm K`, then `\Delta U = -900\ \mathrm J` CORRECT green.)

Answer lock-in: **decrease in internal energy = 900 J**.

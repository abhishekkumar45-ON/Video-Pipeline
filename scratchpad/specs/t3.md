## Question
Two identical perfect black-body plates P and Q in vacuum at fixed T_P > T_Q; the net power per
unit area from P to Q is W₀. Two more identical plates are inserted between them (heat transfers
only between adjacent plates). In steady state the power per unit area from P to Q is W_s.
Find W₀ / W_s.

## KNOWN ANSWER (anchor to this): W₀ / W_s = 3

## Concept-first
- Stefan–Boltzmann net exchange between two black bodies: flux = σ(T_hot⁴ − T_cold⁴).
- In steady state the SAME net flux crosses every gap in a series stack (like equal currents
  through series resistors) — intermediate temperatures self-adjust.

## LIVE VISUAL
Draw 4 vertical plates left→right: P, 1, 2, Q, with equal flux arrows W_s crossing each of the 3
gaps. Label T_P (hot, IGNITION), T_Q (cold, SIGNAL), intermediate T₁, T₂ (TITANIUM). No numeric
ValueTracker needed; the series-of-plates schematic is the visual. Dock bottom-right after.

## STEP RAIL (LAW → SUBSTITUTION → RESULT)
1. Original: **W₀ = σ(T_P⁴ − T_Q⁴)**.
2. After inserting 2 plates → 3 gaps in series, same net flux each:
   `W_s = σ(T_P⁴ − T₁⁴) = σ(T₁⁴ − T₂⁴) = σ(T₂⁴ − T_Q⁴)`.
3. Add the three equal fluxes — the intermediate T₁⁴, T₂⁴ cancel:
   `3 W_s = σ(T_P⁴ − T_Q⁴) = W₀` → **W₀ / W_s = 3**  (general: m plates ⇒ m+1).
   (results rail: `W_0/W_s = 3`, CORRECT green).

Answer lock-in: **W₀ / W_s = 3**.

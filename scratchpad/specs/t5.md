## Question
An ideal gas in equilibrium; a molecule has n degrees of freedom. Internal energy of one mole is
U_n and the speed of sound is v_n. At fixed T and P, which is correct?
- (A) v₃ < v₆ and U₃ > U₆
- (B) v₅ > v₃ and U₃ > U₅
- (C) v₅ > v₇ and U₅ < U₆
- (D) v₆ < v₇ and U₆ < U₇

## KNOWN ANSWER (anchor to this): (C)  v₅ > v₇ and U₅ < U₆

## Concept-first
- Equipartition: U_n = (n/2) R T ⇒ U grows with n.
- Speed of sound v = √(γRT/M), with γ = C_P/C_V = 1 + 2/n ⇒ γ (and v) FALL as n grows.

Left: a molecule icon with degrees of freedom (translation + rotation + vibration). Then two small
"trend" panels: U_n rising bars for n = 3,5,6,7 (IGNITION), and v_n falling bars for n = 3,5,6,7
(SIGNAL). Make the opposite monotonic trends visually obvious. Dock/clear before the solve.

## STEP RAIL (LAW → SUBSTITUTION → RESULT)
1. `U_n = (n/2) R T` increases with n → **U₃ < U₅ < U₆ < U₇**  (so U₅ < U₆ ✓).
2. `v_n = √(γRT/M)`, `γ = 1 + 2/n` decreases with n → **v₃ > v₅ > v₆ > v₇**  (so v₅ > v₇ ✓).
3. Test options: (A) v₃ < v₆ ✗; (B) U₃ > U₅ ✗; (D) v₆ < v₇ ✗; only **(C)** holds:

   (results rail: `v_5>v_7`, `U_5<U_6`).

Answer lock-in: **(C)**.

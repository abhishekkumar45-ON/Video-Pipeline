## Question
An ideal monatomic gas of n moles goes through cycle W→X→Y→Z→W of consecutive
adiabatic and isobaric quasi-static steps, on a schematic V–T diagram. Volumes at
W, X, Y are 64, 125, 250 cm³. Given nRT_W = 1 J. Find the heat absorbed (J) along path X→Y.

## KNOWN ANSWER (anchor everything to this): Q_XY = 1.6 J

## Concept-first (teach before solving)
- Adiabatic (monatomic): T V^(γ−1) = const, with γ = 5/3 so γ−1 = 2/3.
- Isobaric: at constant P, V ∝ T (so V/T = const).
- Isobaric heat: Q = n C_P ΔT, with C_P = 5/2 R for a monatomic gas.

## LIVE VISUAL
Draw the V–T diagram (V vertical or T horizontal — match the "V ∝ T" isobaric ray through the
origin). Plot points W, X, Y. Show W→X as an adiabatic curve and X→Y as a straight isobaric ray.
A live T readout (ValueTracker + DecimalNumber, in units of T_W) as the state moves W→X→Y is a plus.
Then dock the diagram bottom-right.

## STEP RAIL (LAW → SUBSTITUTION → RESULT)
1. Adiabatic W→X: `T_W (64)^{2/3} = T_X (125)^{2/3}` → since 64^{2/3}=16, 125^{2/3}=25 →
   `16 T_W = 25 T_X` → **T_X = (16/25) T_W**.
2. Isobaric X→Y: `V_X/T_X = V_Y/T_Y` → `125/T_X = 250/T_Y` → **T_Y = 2 T_X = (32/25) T_W**.
3. Heat X→Y: `Q = n C_P (T_Y − T_X) = (5/2) nR · (16/25) T_W` → `= (5/2)(16/25)(nR T_W) = (8/5)(1)`
   → **Q_XY = 1.6 J**  (results rail: `Q_{XY}=1.6\ \mathrm J`, CORRECT green).

Answer lock-in: **Q_XY = 1.6 J**.

## Question
A spherical soap bubble inside an air chamber at P₀ = 10⁵ Pa has excess pressure ΔP = 144 Pa.
The chamber pressure is reduced to 8P₀/27; temperature constant, and ΔP ≪ chamber pressure.
Find the new excess pressure ΔP (Pa).

## KNOWN ANSWER (anchor to this): ΔP = 96 Pa

## Concept-first
- Soap bubble excess pressure: ΔP = 4S/r ⇒ ΔP ∝ 1/r (surface tension S fixed).
- Trapped air is isothermal (Boyle): P V = const, with V ∝ r³. Since ΔP ≪ chamber pressure, the
  gas pressure inside ≈ chamber pressure.

## LIVE VISUAL
Draw the chamber (rounded rect, SIGNAL outline) with a soap bubble (circle) inside. As chamber
pressure drops P₀ → 8P₀/27, the bubble RADIUS grows (r₂/r₁ = 3/2). Use a ValueTracker on radius:
animate the circle growing, with live readouts of chamber pressure (in P₀) and ΔP (Pa) —
DecimalNumber, ΔP going 144 → 96. Dock diagram bottom-right after.

## STEP RAIL (LAW → SUBSTITUTION → RESULT)
1. Excess pressure sets radius: **ΔP = 4S/r ⇒ ΔP ∝ 1/r**.
2. Boyle on the trapped air (P ≈ chamber pressure, V ∝ r³):
   `P₀ r₁³ = (8P₀/27) r₂³` → `(r₂/r₁)³ = 27/8` → **r₂/r₁ = 3/2**.
3. New excess pressure `ΔP₂/ΔP₁ = r₁/r₂ = 2/3` → `ΔP₂ = (2/3)(144)` → **ΔP₂ = 96 Pa**
   (results rail: `\Delta P = 96\ \mathrm{Pa}`, CORRECT green).

Answer lock-in: **ΔP = 96 Pa**.
